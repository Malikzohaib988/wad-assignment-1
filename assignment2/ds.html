MS (Data Science)

Evening Programme

Objectives

Data Science has become important due to the need for analyzing and understanding ever-increasing data generated by a multitude of sources, such as online transactions, sensors, social media, and mobile applications.

Quaid-i-Azam University is keen to serve the community and is providing an opportunity to working professionals to enhance their qualifications and knowledge in data science.

Programme Objectives

The main objectives of the programme are to enable students to:

  • Analyze and solve problems related to data using advanced analytical tools.
  • Effectively use appropriate techniques for big data management, data analytics, and data mining.
  • Learn state-of-the-art technologies used in data science.
  • Pursue a PhD degree in data science or a related discipline.

Eligibility

  • Students must have completed 16 years of education in computer science, software engineering, or a related field.

Programme Structure

MS programme consists of 30 credit hours that can be completed in one of the following formats:

  1. 24 credit hours of coursework + 6 credit hours of project/thesis
  2. 30 credit hours of coursework only

Compulsory Courses

DSC-605: Programming for Data Science

Problem solving for data science, programming constructs, data visualization, use of libraries such as NumPy, pandas, and Matplotlib.

DSC-606: Probability and Statistics for Data Science

Probability spaces, random variables, distributions, statistical inference, hypothesis testing, and regression analysis.

Elective Courses

DSC-632: Advanced Web Development

Essentials of web development, JavaScript, server-side scripting, web APIs, frameworks, and performance optimization.

DSC-633: Deep Learning

Neural networks, CNNs, RNNs, autoencoders, generative models, and implementation using TensorFlow/PyTorch.

DSC-634: Big Data Analytics

Techniques and tools for processing big data, Hadoop, Spark, data lakes, NoSQL databases, and distributed computing.

DSC-635: Data Visualization

Principles of data visualization, interactive dashboards, D3.js, Tableau, storytelling with data, and design best practices.

DSC-636: Natural Language Processing

Text processing, tokenization, sentiment analysis, word embeddings, transformers, and applications in real-world tasks.

DSC-637: Data Mining

Clustering, classification, association rule mining, pattern discovery, and practical case studies in various domains.
